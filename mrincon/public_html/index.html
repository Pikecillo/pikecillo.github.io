I am an R&D Software Engineer. I was born in Mérida, Venezuela. I got a BS degree in Systems Engineering from the Universidad de Los Andes in 2007, and a MSc in Computer Science from the University of Houston in 2012.

I worked as a Research Assistance at the UH CGIM Lab, and as a Teaching Assistant at the UH Department of CS, until December 2013. My advisor at UH was Dr. Zhigang Deng. My research interests include Computer Graphics, Computer Animation, GPU Computing, and Human-Computer Interaction.

Since March 2014, I work at HERE Technologies (previously known as Nokia Maps), doing digital map rendering, and navigation systems for the automotive industry.

This is a small gallery of stuff I have done either for courses I've taken or just for fun.

Digital Darkroom

In 2010, I got a copy of the cult book Beyond Photography: The Digital Darkroom by Gerard J. Holzmann (the guy that created the SPIN model checker). I wrote an interpreter of image transformations based on ideas from the book.

This is what happens to Jeff Bezos when he undergoes transformation

new[x, y] = old[rect(1.5 * r ** 2 / R, a)]

You can get the Python source code from my Github accound and try it out. Check the catalog of transformations.

Ray Tracing

I read somewhere that ray tracing is a rite-of-passage in computer graphics. I took it as an invitation as well as a challenge. I wrote from scratch the ray tracer that rendered these images. I learned a lot from the book "Realistic Ray Tracing" by Peter Shirley, and later from a bunch of papers I've ran into. My rite-of-passage isn't over yet.
Depth of field, instancing and noise Motion blur, and more instancing and noise Shadows and one-bounce inter-reflections Reflections and refractions

Boiling water

This is a simulation of boiling water. It is based on a cellular automaton called the Yanagita model. I learned about it in "A Simple Boiling Module", T. Kim, M. Carlson (SCA 2007). The Yanagita model was implemented in 2D, along with a way to visualize the result. The implementation in 3D... I'll finish it when I have some time to spare. Feel free to lend me a couple of coding hands :)
